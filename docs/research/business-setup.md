# Minimum legal setup for a home-based Swiss sole proprietor

Prepared 2026-07-02. Question: what is the legal minimum for the founder (no registered
company, home-based, no separate premises, expected revenue initially well under CHF 100,000)
to start selling lawfully? All thresholds verified against official federal/cantonal sources;
core statute text verbatim from Fedlex as in force 2026-07-02. Items flagged "verify" need
professional confirmation.

**Short answer:** selling can start today. A sole proprietorship exists automatically when
independent economic activity begins - no founding act, no capital, no commercial-register
entry, no VAT number below CHF 100,000 turnover. The genuinely mandatory day-0 items are a
compliant Impressum naming the owner personally with a deliverable postal address, all-inclusive
CHF prices, a privacy statement, the four UWG e-commerce checkout duties, and simple bookkeeping;
promptly after the first sales, AHV self-employment registration (which auto-creates the UID).

## 1. Sole proprietorship by default

- Created automatically when independent, sustainable economic activity begins; no minimum
  capital (kmu.admin.ch). Full personal liability with all private assets.
- Commercial-register entry becomes mandatory only once a financial year closes with revenue
  > = CHF 100,000 (OR Art. 931(1), in force since 2021; the old HRegV Art. 36 was repealed; the
  > often-cited "Art. 934" is pre-2021 numbering). No fixed day-count deadline in statute;
  > enforcement via registry demand, fine up to CHF 5,000.
- Voluntary earlier entry (OR Art. 931(3)) is possible but on balance not advantageous at this
  size: it brings bankruptcy-track debt enforcement (SchKG Art. 39) and strict firm-name use
  duties (OR Art. 954a) for little gain. Cost ~CHF 80.

## 2. Name rules

- Unregistered: he legally trades under his own name; "CUTURA" may be used as a pure brand, but
  legal/business documents and the Impressum must disclose the owner's real name. If ever
  registered, the firm name must contain the family name (OR Art. 945), e.g. "CUTURA Gningue".
- Best practice for the webshop: **"CUTURA, Inhaber: [full name]"**.

## 3. Address in the Impressum

- UWG Art. 3(1)(s) requires clear identity + contact address incl. email (plus the checkout
  duties: contract steps, input-error correction, immediate order confirmation).
- The **private home address is sufficient** - nothing requires commercial premises.
- Email must be shown directly (a contact form does not replace it). A **PO box alone is not
  sufficient** (address must be deliverable).
- Alternative to publishing the home address: a **staffed Domiziladresse / business-address
  service with real mail handling** is treated as compliant by practitioner guides; a bare c/o
  line without mail handling fails. No official SECO guidance / court decision found on this
  exact point - **verify with lawyer** if the home address should stay off the site.

## 4. AHV / social insurance

- Registration with the cantonal SVA/Ausgleichskasse is mandatory as soon as self-employment
  begins; recognition happens retroactively once first invoices exist (register within the
  first weeks; www.selbststaendig-erwerbend.ch).
- Registration **automatically produces the UID** (the Ausgleichskassen are UID offices).
- Sideline (Nebenerwerb) rule: if self-employment is a sideline next to a main job and annual
  income is max **CHF 2,500** (2025/26 value), contributions are levied only on request.
- Rates 2025/26: sliding 5.371-9.321% from CHF 10,100; full 10.0% from CHF 60,500; minimum
  contribution CHF 530. Late surfacing via the tax report costs back-contributions up to 5
  years + 5% default interest - registering on time is strictly cheaper.
- Not applicable to a solo self-employed person: BVG obligation, UVG for himself, ALV. Personal
  accident cover: via an 8h+/week employment's UVG, otherwise include accident coverage in
  health insurance.

## 5. UID + VAT

- **UID is not a precondition to selling.** It is assigned automatically on first announcement
  to a connected authority (AHV registration, VAT registration, or HR entry).
- VAT: exempt below CHF 100,000 worldwide turnover (MWSTG Art. 10). Once liable,
  self-registration within 30 days (Art. 66).
- **Voluntary registration (Art. 11)** is the only way to reclaim the 8.1% import VAT on the
  Vietnam shipments (Art. 28(1)(c)); use the effective method (NOT Saldosteuersatz, which bars
  separate input-tax recovery). Honest economics: for a profitable all-inclusive-price B2C
  seller, staying exempt is usually cheaper; registration pays off in the loss-making launch
  phase (net refunds) and avoids a later pricing discontinuity. **Run the numbers with a
  fiduciary.** If registered: invoices must show CHE-xxx.xxx.xxx MWST.
- Imports: garments from Vietnam are duty-free (industrial tariffs abolished 1.1.2024); 8.1%
  import VAT on value incl. transport; Post/courier handles the customs declaration for
  parcels; register the UID in the BAZG Zollkundenverwaltung to retrieve electronic
  assessments (eVV) once reclaiming.

## 6. Other minimums

- **No permits** for an online clothing shop as such (ZH business portal); Product Safety Act
  applies generally.
- **Insurance is optional** (liability recommended). Check cantonal movables fire-insurance
  specifics.
- **Bookkeeping:** simplified "Milchbuechlein" regime below CHF 500,000 revenue (OR Art.
  957(2)): chronological income/expenses with receipts + year-end asset position; 10-year
  retention; profit declared in the private tax return.
- **Home business in a rented flat:** no landlord consent needed while the use stays quiet and
  administrative (no regular customer visits, employees, signage, or warehouse character).
- **Textile labelling:** voluntary in CH (declared content must be truthful under UWG); GINETEX
  care symbols are a private licensing standard. EEA/Liechtenstein angle not researched -
  verify before selling to LI.
- **Privacy statement is required** (DSG Art. 19); no DPO needed; processing register waived
  under 250 employees (absent large-scale sensitive processing).
- **AGB are not statutory but strongly recommended** (and per project policy lawyer-signed).

## Day-0 minimum checklist (this exact case)

1. Nothing to found - start selling; accept unlimited personal liability. (Recommended: a
   separate bank account, which also serves as SVA evidence.)
2. Publish the Impressum: brand + full name + deliverable postal address (home address is
   sufficient; Domiziladresse as the privacy-preserving alternative, lawyer-confirm) + email.
3. Checkout compliance per UWG 3(1)(s) - already built into the shop.
4. All-inclusive CHF prices (PBV) - already policy.
5. Privacy statement on the site.
6. Truthful product claims incl. fibre composition.
7. Simple books from day 1 (receipts, 10-year retention).
8. Within the first weeks: SVA self-employment registration (auto-UID). Sideline waiver
   (<= CHF 2,500/year profit) only on request.
9. Decide voluntary VAT registration with a fiduciary (import-VAT recovery vs 8.1% on sales).
10. Can wait: commercial-register entry (>= CHF 100k), liability insurance, trademark for
    "CUTURA", landlord notice.

Impressum block to publish now (no company, no UID yet):

```
Impressum
CUTURA
Inhaber: [full name]
[Strasse Nr.]
[PLZ Ort], Schweiz
E-Mail: [address]
```

Optional later additions: phone; "UID: CHE-xxx.xxx.xxx"; if VAT-registered
"MWST-Nr. CHE-xxx.xxx.xxx MWST".
