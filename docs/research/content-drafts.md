## 1. FAQ

### How does made-to-measure work at CUTURA?

You choose a model, fabric, and style details, then enter your measurements. Your garment is cut and sewn to those measurements by our tailoring partner in Vietnam, quality-checked in Switzerland, and shipped to you. Nothing is pulled from a shelf; each piece is made for your order.

### Do I need a tailor to measure me?

No. You can measure yourself at home with a soft tape measure, and our measuring guide walks you through each step. If you would rather have a tailor take your measurements, that works too. Either way, accurate measurements are what make the fit right, so it is worth taking your time.

### How do I measure myself accurately?

Follow our measuring guide step by step. Use a soft tape, keep it level and snug but not tight, and measure twice. If two readings differ, measure again. Measuring over a well-fitting garment or close to the skin, as each step instructs, helps. For back and shoulder measurements, ask someone to help you.

### What if it does not fit?

Every first garment is covered by our Fit Promise. If the fit of your first shirt or first trousers is not right, contact us within 30 days with a few photos. We review it personally and remake the garment free of charge with corrected measurements, and you keep the original. If a remake still cannot get the fit right, you receive a full refund. This is a voluntary promise we make in addition to your statutory rights.

### Can I return it or change my mind?

Made-to-measure garments are produced individually for you, so under Swiss law they are non-returnable for change of mind, and there is no statutory right of withdrawal for online purchases in Switzerland. What we do offer is the Fit Promise on your first shirt and first trousers, which covers fit specifically. If a garment has a genuine production fault, it is always covered under statutory law.

### How long does it take?

Each garment is made to order in Vietnam and quality-checked in Switzerland before it ships, so it takes longer than an off-the-shelf item. You will get tracking once your order ships.

Yes. We can send you fabric swatches so you can see and feel the material and check the colour in your own light before you order. [SWATCHES: confirm whether free or paid, and how to request.]

### How do I care for my garment?

Care instructions come with each garment and follow the international care symbols on the sewn-in label. In general, wash cool, avoid harsh cycles, and iron at the temperature the fabric allows. Following the care guidance keeps the fit and finish over time. [CARE DETAIL: confirm per-fabric specifics if needed.]

### How do I pay?

You can pay with TWINT or with a credit or debit card. Payment is handled on a secure hosted checkout, and we never see or store your card details.

### Is my data safe? Are my measurements safe?

Yes. Your measurements are encrypted and stored on servers in Switzerland or the EU. We keep only what we need to make and support your order, and we never share your measurements for advertising or profiling.

### Where are the clothes made?

CUTURA is designed in Switzerland and every garment is quality-checked in Switzerland before it ships. The garments themselves are made by our tailoring partner in Vietnam, a workshop we have chosen for its craft and consistency. We are specific about this on purpose: it is honest, and the Swiss quality check is a real step, not a label.

### Can I reorder in my saved size?

Yes. Your measurements are saved to your account, so you can reorder the same or a different model without measuring again. If your body has changed, update your measurements first so the new garment reflects them.

### What sizes and body types do you fit?

Because every garment is made to your own measurements rather than to standard sizes, we fit a wide range of body types. There are no fixed size labels to fall between. If your measurements fall outside what we can currently produce well, we will tell you before you order rather than guess.

### What if I measured wrong?

It happens, and it is exactly what the Fit Promise is there for on your first shirt and first trousers. If the fit is off, send us a few photos within 30 days; we review it, remake the garment with corrected measurements at no charge, and you keep the original. A careful first measurement is still the best way to get it right the first time, which is why our measuring guide is detailed.

### Can I choose how the garment fits, not just the size?

Where a model offers it, you can choose a fit intent such as slim, regular, or relaxed. This shapes how close the garment sits to your body while still being built to your measurements. If you are unsure, regular is a safe starting point.

### Do you ship outside Switzerland?

At launch we ship within Switzerland. Liechtenstein may follow. See our Shipping and delivery page for current details.

### How is the price shown?

The price you see is the price you pay. It already includes standard shipping and Swiss VAT. There is nothing added at checkout except an optional paid express method, if you choose it.

## 2. How to measure (fit guide)

Good measurements are the single most important step in getting a made-to-measure garment right the first time. Take your time. This guide takes about ten minutes.

### Before you start

- Use a soft measuring tape (the flexible fabric or plastic kind, not a rigid builder's tape).
- Wear light clothing, or measure close to the skin where a step says so.
- Stand relaxed and upright, looking straight ahead. Do not hold your breath or puff out your chest.
- Keep the tape level all the way around, and snug but not tight. You should be able to slip a finger under it.
- Measure each value twice. If the two readings differ, measure a third time and use the consistent one.
- Ask someone to help with back, shoulder, and sleeve measurements. They are hard to take accurately alone.
- Record in centimetres.

### A useful shortcut: measure a garment that already fits

For several measurements you can measure a shirt or pair of trousers you already own that fits you the way you like, laid flat on a table. Where a step allows this, we say so. This is often more repeatable than measuring your body, especially for width. Choose a garment in good shape, button or zip it, and smooth out the wrinkles before measuring.

### Fit intent

Where a model offers it, you can pick a fit intent:

- Slim: closer to the body, less room through the chest, waist, and legs.
- Regular: a balanced, everyday fit with natural room to move.
- Relaxed: more room throughout, easier over the shoulders and midsection.

Pick the one that matches how you like to wear the piece. If unsure, choose regular.

### Body vs garment

Some measurements are of your body, and some describe the finished garment. We handle the difference for you: enter the values as the guide asks, and our system adds the right amount of room (ease) for the model and fit intent you chose. You do not need to add anything extra yourself. If you add your own ease on top, the garment will come out too large.

### Shirt measurements

1. Neck: Measure around the base of your neck, where a collar sits, keeping the tape level. Add a little room so it is comfortable to close; do not measure tight.
2. Chest: Measure around the fullest part of your chest, under the arms and across the shoulder blades, keeping the tape level.
3. Waist (shirt): Measure around your natural waist, roughly at the narrowest point above your navel. Keep the tape level and relaxed.
4. Hip / seat: Measure around the fullest part of your seat, keeping the tape level.
5. Shoulder width: With help, measure across your back from the edge of one shoulder to the edge of the other, following the line where a shoulder seam would sit.
6. Sleeve length: With your arm slightly bent, measure from the shoulder point, along the outside of the arm past the elbow, to the wrist bone.
7. Shirt length: From the base of the back of your neck straight down to where you want the shirt to end.
8. Bicep: Measure around the fullest part of your upper arm.
9. Wrist: Measure around your wrist where a cuff would close.

Tip: For chest, waist, and hip, measuring a well-fitting shirt laid flat and doubling the flat width is a good cross-check against your body measurement. If the two disagree a lot, measure both again.

### Trouser measurements

1. Waist (trouser): Measure around where you actually wear your trousers, which may be at or slightly below your natural waist. Keep the tape level and relaxed, not tight.
2. Seat / hip: Measure around the fullest part of your seat, keeping the tape level and your feet together.
3. Inseam: Measure from the crotch straight down the inside of the leg to where you want the trouser to end (at the ankle, or the top of the shoe). Measuring a pair that fits, laid flat, is often easiest here.
4. Outseam: Measure from your waistband line down the outside of the leg to the same end point.
5. Thigh: Measure around the fullest part of your upper leg.
6. Knee: Measure around your leg at the knee.
7. Leg opening / hem: This is a garment value. Measure the opening on a pair you like, laid flat, and double it, or tell us how wide you want the hem.
8. Front rise: Measure from the crotch seam up to the top of the waistband at the front. Measuring a well-fitting pair laid flat is easiest.

Tip: Trouser waist and inseam are where most fit issues start. Re-check both. If your favourite trousers sit lower than your natural waist, measure at that lower line and note it.

### After you measure

- Read your values back once. Numbers that look far outside the usual range are usually a slipped tape; re-measure those.
- Save your measurements to your account so you can reorder without measuring again.
- If a value still feels uncertain, tell us in the order notes. We would rather ask than guess.

If measuring at home does not work for you for any reason, a local tailor can take these measurements in a few minutes.
