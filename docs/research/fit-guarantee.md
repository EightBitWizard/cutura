# Fit / remake guarantee - legal and business research

Prepared 2026-07-01. Topic: whether and how to offer a fit/remake guarantee for made-to-measure
shirts and trousers sold online to consumers in CH (+ LI).

**One-line answer:** Yes, promise a remake. A bounded, remake-first fit guarantee is the market
norm for online made-to-measure, it is legally safe in Switzerland if worded as a voluntary
goodwill promise on top of statutory rights, and the true remake rate is low (~1-5%) if the
measuring guide is good.

## Legal framing (Switzerland)

- A made-to-measure garment is most likely a **work contract (Werkvertrag, OR Art. 363 ff)**
  rather than a plain sale; the exact classification (Werkvertrag vs sale of a thing to be
  produced) is a genuine legal question - confirm with a Swiss lawyer. Either regime gives the
  customer statutory defect rights already. A garment sewn correctly to the measurements the
  customer entered, that then does not fit because of self-measurement error, is generally NOT a
  statutory defect - that is exactly the gap a voluntary guarantee fills.
- **No statutory withdrawal right** in CH, and custom goods are doubly excluded, so the customer
  has no "changed my mind" return. The guarantee is therefore the trust mechanism, not a nicety.
- **Gewaehrleistung (statutory) vs Garantie (voluntary):** a voluntary guarantee supplements and
  must never push the customer below the statutory minimum (2-year consumer warranty, OR Art.
  210(4), cannot be shortened).
- **How to word it safely:** label it voluntary and additional; state that statutory rights
  remain unaffected; never phrase it as the customer's "only remedy"; keep bounds (window, once,
  photos) as conditions on the extra goodwill, not as cuts to the statutory floor (UWG Art. 8
  bans consumer terms that create a significant, unjustified imbalance contrary to good faith);
  be transparent and specific; separate "fit goodwill" from "statutory defect"; promise the
  process, not a guaranteed result; version the text and record the accepted version per order;
  get the final de/fr/it wording lawyer-signed.

## What competitors offer (market norm)

- A **free remake if the fit is wrong is effectively universal** among online self-measurement
  made-to-measure brands. Not offering one would put CUTURA below the market.
- Two dominant models: **(A) keep-the-original, free remake, no cash refund** (Tailor Store, Son
  of a Tailor) - simplest, most trust-building, no reverse logistics; **(B) return-to-remake with
  refund and/or local-alteration credit** (Proper Cloth refund; Sumissura/Hockerty/Indochino
  alter-first, remake only if unalterable).
- Cash refunds are the minority (Proper Cloth, tied to return). Near-universal bounds:
  first-order-only, once per garment type. Windows cluster at 30-90 days. Photos + a review step
  are standard. Alteration-credit-first (Indochino/Hockerty) is a cost-control variant that needs
  a local-tailor network CUTURA does not have.
- **CUTURA's intended policy (remake-first, money-back fallback, keep the original) is at or
  slightly above the generous end of the market** - a genuine trust differentiator, and
  affordable because the remake rate is low.

## Risk / cost

- Fit drives ~70% of apparel returns; made-to-measure attacks that. Reported true full-remake
  rates are low single digits with good measurement support (one remote MTM tailor reports ~97%
  fit accuracy, remakes <1 in 100; showroom MTM sees 15-25% alterations but few full remakes).
- Expect CUTURA's true remake rate to be low single digits (roughly 3-6% early, trending to
  1-3%) with a strong measuring guide; trousers remake more than shirts.
- Biggest lever to keep the guarantee cheap: **a strong measuring guide**, size/fit confirmation
  before production (the founder approval gate can double as fit review), conservative ease
  defaults, an explicit fit-intent choice, a saved measurement profile, and photo-gated review.
- "Keep the original" costs one extra production run per remake with no salvage, but removes
  reverse logistics and is a strong trust signal; bounded (first order, once per type) it is
  affordable at CUTURA's volumes.

## Recommended policy (the "confident but bounded" model)

Offer a remake-first fit guarantee. Customer keeps the original. Money-back only as a
founder-approved fallback when a remake will not solve it.

| Parameter           | Value                                                                                                                            |
| ------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Eligibility         | First order per garment type (first shirt, first trousers), once                                                                 |
| Window              | Report within 30 days of delivery (be generous within 30 days)                                                                   |
| Evidence            | Photos required (garment worn: front, back, problem area) + optional measurement re-check                                        |
| Review              | Founder-reviewed, human decision                                                                                                 |
| Primary remedy      | Free remake (fresh production run, corrected measurements)                                                                       |
| Keep original?      | Yes, at launch                                                                                                                   |
| Fallback remedy     | Full money-back if a remake cannot reasonably solve the fit, at CUTURA's discretion                                              |
| Exclusions          | Change of mind, body/weight change, damage/wear/washing beyond care, self-altered garments, any second+ order of the same type   |
| Statutory carve-out | Genuine production faults (wrong fabric, sewing fault, spec mismatch) handled under statutory law WITHOUT the 30-day/once limits |

**Alternatives:** (A) more generous - widen the window to 60 days (keep the refund fallback);
(B) more cautious - alteration-credit-first for minor issues (Indochino/Hockerty model), best
held in reserve as a lever if trouser remake rates run high, since launching with it would
undercut the trust position.

## Exact public-facing wording (English master; translate to de/fr/it)

**Short version (product page / checkout / confirmation):**

> **The CUTURA Fit Promise.** Your first shirt and your first pair of trousers are covered by
> our Fit Promise. If the fit is not right, tell us within 30 days and send a few photos. We
> will remake the garment for you, free of charge, and you keep the original. If a remake still
> cannot get the fit right, we will refund you in full. This is our voluntary promise in
> addition to your rights under Swiss law, which remain unaffected.

**Full version (guarantee page / terms):**

> **The CUTURA Fit Promise**
>
> Every CUTURA garment is made to your measurements. We want it to fit, so we back your first
> order with our Fit Promise. This is a voluntary promise we give you in addition to your
> statutory rights under Swiss law. Your legal rights are not limited by anything below.
>
> **What is covered.** Your first shirt and your first pair of trousers. The Fit Promise can be
> used once for each of these.
>
> **How it works.**
>
> 1. If the fit is not right, contact us within 30 days of receiving your order.
> 2. Send us a few clear photos of the garment worn - front, back, and the area that does not
>    fit. We may ask you to check one or two measurements with us.
> 3. We review your request personally and, together with you, decide what to change.
> 4. We remake the garment for you, free of charge, with corrected measurements. You keep the
>    original - wear it, alter it, or pass it on.
> 5. If a remake still cannot achieve a good fit, we will refund you in full.
>
> **What is not covered.** A change of mind, a change in your body or weight after you ordered,
> normal wear, damage, care not followed, or a garment that has been altered elsewhere. The Fit
> Promise applies to your first garment of each type; further orders are not covered by it.
>
> **Production faults are always covered.** If a garment reaches you with a genuine fault - the
> wrong fabric, a sewing defect, or a garment that does not match the specification you confirmed
>
> - that is not subject to the 30-day or once-only limits above. Please contact us and we will
>   put it right under your statutory rights.
>
> **Your legal rights.** The Fit Promise is a voluntary, additional service. It does not replace
> or reduce your statutory rights under Swiss law, which continue to apply in full.
